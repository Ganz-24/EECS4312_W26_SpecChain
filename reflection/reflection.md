# Application: [Medito]

## Most Important Difference Between Pipelines
The most important difference between the pipelines was the level of grounding in the review data. The automated pipeline produced broader personas and requirements that were loosely connected to the review groups, while the manual pipeline produced more focused personas that were clearly tied to specific user feedback. The hybrid pipeline reduced this gap by refining the automated outputs and ensuring that each persona, requirement, and test remained directly traceable to the original review evidence.

## Most Useful Pipeline
The hybrid pipeline was the most useful overall. It combined the efficiency of the automated pipeline with the precision of the manual pipeline. The automated outputs provided a strong starting point, but required refinement to remove ambiguity and unsupported assumptions. After refinement, the hybrid pipeline produced clear, testable requirements and well-aligned tests without requiring the full effort of manual construction.

## Most Surprising Finding
The most surprising finding was that many automated requirements appeared correct at a surface level but were not usable for validation. Although they were grammatically well-formed, they often contained vague terms such as “easy to use” or “high quality,” which made them difficult to test. This showed that correctness in wording does not guarantee clarity or testability in requirements engineering.

## Observed Weakness in the Automated Pipeline
A major weakness in the automated pipeline was the presence of unsupported assumptions in the personas and vague requirements. Some personas included characteristics that were not clearly derived from the review groups, and many requirements lacked measurable or observable conditions. This led to test cases that were either unclear or not directly connected to specific system behavior, reducing the overall reliability of the outputs.0
